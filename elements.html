Research at Pearl Academy

Our focus is to create a favorable research environment at Pearl Academy by creating a Focused Research Group utilizing the benefit of the research for the development of society and a larger global community. It promotes a culture of achieving academic excellence in accordance with research governance and ethical practices.

Research-led teaching is integral to our institution and the scope of our research is aligned with three themes: 

  • AI-driven aesthetics: The art of intelligent design
  • Design futuristics: focusing on aesthetics, style, functionality, and efficiency. 
  • Craftivism: sustainability of craft communities and environment

Research framework 

Collaborative space for emerging and established researchers 

This space provides connection, motivation, and solutions for emerging researchers who are our undergraduate and postgraduate students and established researchers from industry and academia. This space is an open platform for research scholars from all campuses to come together and share their research ideas and develop innovative projects. 

Supporting researchers across faculty and students 

Research Cell facilitates our faculty and students to get involved in research by identifying platforms to showcase their work and publish their articles to develop a quality of work that is interdisciplinary and will support their career development.

Research training is organised by the Research cell to develop the faculty's understanding of the key principles of research integrity and responsibilities, planning projects, identifying methodologies, and decimation of findings. 

To achieve the outcomes the research and innovation process at Pearl Academy includes:

  • Diversity and inclusiveness:

    Research at Pearl Academy looks to produce outcomes that align with the values and expectations of society and all groups involved in and affected by the research and innovation need to work together. Inclusivity of perspectives and expertise to generate higher quality of study and ensure all points of view are considered. 

  • Transparency and openness:

    Research at Pearl Academy is driven by achieving a more knowledge-based society. This means making the process of research and innovation more transparent and open to all, providing meaningful information at all stages of the process to encourage engagement with discussion and scrutiny, which allows faculty and students to make more informed decisions.

  • Anticipation and Reflection:

    Research at Pearl Academy impacts the body of work and its influence on the future in the specialist area. This considers a desirable future anticipating the possible impacts reflecting on the underlying assumptions, values, and purposes of research and innovation that generate insights that allow more responsible action. 

  • Responsive and adaptive to change:

    Research and innovation must respond to the views expressed by stakeholders and, if necessary, methods or goals could change thereof. The ability to adapt because of different views, changing circumstances, or fresh knowledge is an important element of research at Pearl Academy

At Pearl Academy, we appreciate that research defines and drives both design and learning and for the betterment of society, is central to our mission and the achievement of our vision as design educators. We encourage research and value its importance in growing knowledge and information, and therefore encourage and fully support both colleagues and students who undertake it.

Placement Structure

  1. 1. Career Empowerment Sessions Participating in Career Empowerment Sessions is essential for students as they delve into vital aspects like interview etiquette, workplace dos and don'ts, appropriate attire, email etiquette, and professional conduct. These sessions also feature industry experts who share invaluable insights and guidance on these crucial topics, preparing students for success in their careers.
  2. 2. Resume Screening & Approval During our sessions, we help students create their resumes by sharing current trends and formats, boosting their chances of being shortlisted by companies. Subsequently, our career services department meticulously reviews and vets each resume before presenting profiles to companies.
  3. 3. Industry Knowledge AssessmentThis comprehensive test evaluates your ability to analyze industry data, identify market trends, and make informed business decisions. By taking this test, you will gain valuable insights into the dynamic landscape of various industries, enhancing your career readiness and competitiveness in the job market. Join us to test your industry acumen and unlock new opportunities for growth and success in your chosen field.
  4. 4. Career Preferences ProfileStudents complete a personalized career profile detailing their areas of interest, preferred locations, desired industries, and dream companies they aspire to join.
  5. 5.Practice Interview Session Mock interviews provide a simulated real-life experience to aid students in refining their interview skills. Participants gain insights into resume and portfolio presentation, as well as the art of responding professionally to interview questions, enhancing their readiness for actual job interviews.
  6. 6.Placements opportunities.Employers collaborate with us to offer job openings. Some companies arrange pre-placement talks, while others conduct direct interviews. Interviews can take place on-campus, off-campus at the company's premises, or virtually.

1. Portal Orientation

Introducing students to the placement portal. The placement portal is an online forum where all jobs for campus placements are posted and processed.

2. Portal Registration

Following the portal orientation, students receive their sign in credentials on the PA email id. The students register on the portal with their basic information and also upload their resumes and portfolio links.

3. Professional Development Sessions

Professional Development sessions are a must for students to attend. The sessions primarily cover interview skills, dos and donts on the job. Basic dressing for interviews and post joining. Email etiquettes, professional behaviour at the workplace. During these sessions we also invite industry experts to speak to the students on these topics.

4. Resume Vetting & Approvals

During our session with the students, we support them in making their resumes. We share current resume trends and formats to ensure our students have best chances during resume shortlisting by companies. Post the students uploading the resumes on the portal, the career service department checks and vets the resumes before finally sharing profiles with the companies.

5. Industry awareness test

An industry awareness test is conducted, the test has questions about the industry and current trends. It primarily tries to cover questions that could be asked during the interviews.

6. Profiling sheet

Students fill a profiling sheet where they mention their interest areas, location preferences, industry preferences and dream companies that they would like to work with.

7. Mock interviews

Mock interviews are conducted to help prepare students better for the real life situation. This helps students understand how to present their resume and portfolio. Also, learn how to answer questions professionally during the interview.

8. Campus placements

Companies reach out to us with job opportunities. Some companies conduct a pre-placement talk whereas, some directly conduct interviews. Interviews are either conducted on-campus, off-campus i.e., at the companys location or virtually.

1. Pre-Placement Talk

As part of our placement drive, we welcome distinguished companies to our campus to present information about their organization, job roles, and compensation packages. This equips our students with the knowledge to make well-informed career decisions.

2. Portfolio Shortlisting

After shortlisting resumes, the pivotal phase in the selection process involves carefully assessing portfolios.Students are encouraged to highlight their distinct talents and abilities through their portfolio submissions.

3. Assessment Review

Top companies evaluate students' creativity and time management through timed assignments, allowing them to demonstrate their abilities and stand out.

4. Resume Shortlisting

Once resumes are shortlisted, the next vital step in the selection process is reviewing the portfolios. It is essential for students to highlight their unique talents and skills in their resume.

5. Personal Interview

Personal interviews evaluate candidates' skills, experiences, and fit for the industry, ensuring they are prepared and confident for their future roles.This process helps students showcase their problem-solving abilities and communication skills.

6. Final offer

Upon successful evaluation, the official offer and confirmation are communicated to both the Career Service Department and the selected students.

Arts University Bournemouth, UK

Founded in 1880 as the Bournemouth Government School of Art, Arts University Bournemouth has evolved over time to encompass science, design, and technology. In 2012, it achieved full university status, specialising in art, performance, design, and media within the creative industries. The university offers a comprehensive range of programs at the graduate, postgraduate, and research levels, including a variety of short courses. With approximately 20 undergraduate and 10 graduate courses, the institution is divided into two faculties: Art and Design, and Media and Performance. Distinguished by its specialised focus, Arts University Bournemouth stands as one of the few institutions in the UK devoted to fostering a creative environment. Here, faculty and students share a profound enthusiasm for Arts and Design. The University for the Creative Arts is positioned at #71 in The Complete University Guide List 2023. According to the Guardian University Rankings 2023, it claims the 70th spot in the rankings.
